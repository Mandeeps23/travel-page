Dish of the Month — July 2026
Tonkotsu Ramen,
Tokyo
Deep Dive

Eighteen hours of devotion

In the back alleys of Shinjuku, a single bowl of tonkotsu ramen represents a craft passed down through generations. The broth alone demands eighteen hours of simmering — pork bones coaxed into a silk-white emulsion that coats the mouth without heaviness. Every element is deliberate: noodles calibrated to the broth's viscosity, tare measured by eye, toppings arranged in silence.

Fukuoka, Kyushu, Japan
18+ hours
Winter (Dec–Feb)
